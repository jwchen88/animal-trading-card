Moose

Moose usually have just one or two babies, known as calves.

  • Scientific Name: Alces alces
  • Height: 4.6 – 6.9 ft. (Adult, At Shoulder)
  • Mass: Male: 840 – 1,500 lbs (Adult), Female: 440 – 790 lbs (Adult)
  • Diet: terrestrial and aquatic vegetation

The moose or elk, Alces alces, is the largest extant species in the deer family. Moose are distinguished by the broad, flat antlers of the males; other members of the family have antlers with a dendritic configuration.
